Financial Plan

7 Basic steps for your financial plan

1. Plan out where your money goes

Plan out your expenditures and where your money goes. Don't worry about trying to cut down on your money spending, just calculate your usage and monitor it weekly and monthly. Use a document on your computer or journal to keep track of your spending.

2. Make personal finance goals

Make specific goals for yourself like "I want to earn 10$ from babysitting", or small jobs you may have. Make goals on how much money you want to earn up to a specific date.

3. Keep an eye on what you buy, and your credit

Follow these goals, and now is where you monitor your expenditures and savings. A big factor is being consistent with your plans.

4. Start saving

This is when you seriously monitor your savings, and see if you have reached your levels and limits within the time frame you allotted for this goal..

5. Start budgeting

Use these savings and divide them up into your edited spending's. This is the part of your plan where you are most organized and see your plans very clear.

6. Plan for when you have to spend the money you have saved for specific purposes

Now that you have divided your saving into categories, such as savings, spending for specific purposes, etc., plan for when you have to spend this amount of money.

7. Keep track and follow your plan

Lastly, the easiest step, is to follow your plan consistently, and over time you will be organized for your future. If you prepare now, in the future bigger expenses like bills and taxes and more confusing topics will be a breeze to categorize, plan for, and pay.

Bugeting

    Budgets are an important part of your money process. You can stay on top and organized if you have a specific plan and steps in which to accomplish your goals. To begin you're going to want to calculate how much you spend and on what you spend that money on. Also check your income regularly to see just how much you are earning. Check your debt and payoff goals, plus things you wish to save for. Allocate money for everything you need to pay off, or are saving for. Record your budget so that you remember to add or take away money when needed, and when you have reached your goals. Breaking your budget from time to time is ok, just remember to get back on track as soon as possible.

 Income

    When people receive income, they have provided a good or service, and earned wages by investing. This income(money) can come in the form of salaries, wages, etc. Individuals also get taxed from their income, such as the individual income tax. You can set financial goals into how you want to divide your money so you can be prepared. For example, you might want to have an emergency fund, savings, spending money, plus tax money and money for bills is being deducted. When planning by yourself, it's very important to keep a record of all of your income and where it is going.

 Savings

    Deciding to put a decent amount your income aside for saving is a smart idea, for the more you save, you will have on hand for emergencies and special funds. To save smart, try avoiding impulsive spending. Research from the University of Pennsylvania shows that people who can avoid impulse spending can save up to 23% on their grocery bills. Also, people tend to be a bit more excessive with money when they shop with a card because you are not in control of how much money you are using, whereas with hard cash you are able to see and keep a check on how much money you use on an item. Make sure to keep track of sales and coupons. Remember to pay off your debts early on so you don't have to worry about them in the longer run. Always keep your loose change, and keep track of your saving account at the bank regularly.
